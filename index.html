Who I am

Grounded in
community

I'm the eldest daughter of Vietnamese boat people, born and raised in public housing and public schools across the western suburbs of Naarm (Melbourne). I know firsthand what it means to rely on public systems — and to fight for the people who depend on them.

My career hasn't been linear — and that's my greatest asset. I've been a senior public servant, a State MP (Greens, Western Metro), a bilingual union organiser on poultry factory floors, a grassroots facilitator for young people of colour, a federal election candidate, and a local mutual aid worker — all while raising two kids in Sunshine North.

I have ADHD, which means I've built systems, processes, and accountability structures not because I was told to, but because I had to. The same goes for trauma — I've learnt the hard way how to build resilience and trust in teams and communities, and I bring that wisdom into every room I enter.

I believe our systems aren't broken — they were designed to keep us distracted and divided. Solidarity in collective action is our answer. That belief drives every piece of work I do.

I'm also an artist. Watercolour, pen and ink, graphic recording. Because sometimes the most subversive thing you can do is make something beautiful.

What I've made

Here's a taste of what happens when I show up to the work. Real outcomes, real communities, real change.

Local Government · 2011–2018

Award-Winning Sustainability Team at Brimbank Council

Sustainability Coordinator — 5 FTE team lead

Led an award-winning team of environmental educators and project officers for seven years, managing $1.4M in annual operations and $3.5M in capital works. Coordinated complex multi-stakeholder programs including Brimbank's Streetlight LED Replacement program and multiple stormwater harvesting projects. Built a team culture that thrived through major budget cycles, job shares, and shifting staffing profiles without losing momentum or quality.

Impact Won a Sustainability Victoria award for the Brimbank Gems recycling behaviour change program — which was then adopted and run by councils across the country.

Federal Election · 2024–2025

Second Highest Greens Swing in the Country — Fraser

Greens Federal Candidate for Fraser, VIC

As candidate for one of Australia's most diverse electorates, built a campaign from the ground up — bilingual content (Vietnamese-English), social media end-to-end (scripting, filming, editing, publishing), community forums, and deep grassroots engagement across faith communities, multicultural organisations, and local groups. Grew the campaign's Instagram to 2,500 followers — more than the incumbent Labor MP — in under 12 months.

Impact Achieved the second highest swing to the Greens of any electorate across the country at the 2025 Federal Election.

Movement Building · 2021–Present

Climate Justice Fellowship — Democracy in Colour

Facilitator & Curriculum Designer

Developed and delivered bespoke organiser training programs for young people of colour from across Australia — 2022, 2023, and 2025 cohorts. Topics included activist burnout, power mapping, grassroots organising 101, 'loving corrections' (radical feedback), and consensus decision-making. Designed to equip participants to return to their home communities and run campaigns grounded in their own lived experience and intersecting identities.

Impact Equipped dozens of young activists of colour from across the country to lead their own community campaigns on climate, racial, and economic justice.
